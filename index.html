Bonjour à toutes et à tous!

Although New Mexico is home to a unique intersection of Hispanophone and indigenous cultures, French and Francophone culture has also had an enduring impact on the state.

Learn more

Did you know Nobel Prize-winning author J.M.G. Le Clézio has taught at the University of New Mexico (UNM) three academic years? Over the years he has made Albuquerque his second home, and in fact, his last stay here was in 2008, just two months before he was named a Nobel Laureate.Local bookstores such as Page 1 Books and even the Albuquerque Public Library system have modest selections of French-language texts by Le Clézio and other Francophone authors.

Please see the following section for other ways to get involved with the French-speaking community in Albuquerque.

Central New Mexico Community College offers a handful of French classes that can be taken for credit.

In addition to classes for degree-seeking students, the University of New Mexico also offers Continuing Education classes in French each semester.

Between Albuquerque and Santa Fe, there are four active French conversation groups. Check out the current offerings and find one that's convenient for you.

The Alliance Française is an international organization that promotes the French language and Francophone culture. There are 110 chapters in the United States, and Albuquerque is lucky to have one of them.
